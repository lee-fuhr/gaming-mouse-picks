What polling rate do I need for gaming?

Most gaming mice offer polling rates from 125 Hz to 1000 Hz. For competitive gamers, 1000 Hz (1 ms response) is standard and more than enough. Higher rates like 4000 Hz or 8000 Hz are available on some newer models but provide diminishing returns and use more CPU power. You wonâ€™t notice a difference beyond 1000 Hz in typical gameplay.

Absolutely. Many gaming mice have programmable buttons that can be mapped to copy/paste, volume, or application shortcuts. Their precise sensors also work well on any surface. However, some may lack a quiet click or the ergonomic sculpt of an office mouse, so consider that for all-day typing work.

How long do gaming mouse switches typically last?

Modern switches are rated for 50 to 100 million clicks. Optical switches last longer because they use light instead of physical contact. In real-world use, a good mouse should last several years before switch degradation appears. Double-click issues can sometimes be fixed with software debounce settings or warranty replacement.
